Post 3: Dates No Longer Included

Another one from the archive—my first multi-strand—well, 2-strand—design. For many years, I exclusively designed and tied knots from single pieces of string. Hour after hour, I toiled away, confined rigidly in my single-strand fundamentalism—as my designs grew larger and more complex, I grew increasingly frustrated with my self-imposed limitations, but still I refused to break the mold, ridiculously clinging to the conviction that multi-strand knots were in some sense filthy, impure, and unworthy of my time and effort.

How wrong I was! This view hinged on the misconception that an extra strand would add nothing to a design beyond the vile promiscuity of extra running ends. This changed, however, when I arrived at the long-overdue realization that each new strand, if worked into the design intelligently and carefully, in fact had the potential to add an entire new axis of symmetry. And suddenly, the multi-strand technique was transformed in my mind from a form of cheap, loathsome harlotry to a noble, pure, and necessary form of abstraction. Making sense? No? That's ok—I wouldn't expect a mind as feeble as yours, dear nonexistent reader, to follow my drunken, late-night/early morning ramblingsthe fabulous intricacies of my imposing genius.
